User problems

  1. 1 Users can't easily navigate complex tables of data with no visual hierarchy.
  2. 2 The current interface was too rigid and data was cut off or hidden from users.
The solution

Using color, type hierarchy, and iconography, I visually pulled out actionable insights. I built variable layouts that could respond to many types of data, and a central component library that the design team could use to quickly mock up designs.

My design approach

Due to the confidential nature of Elemica's enterprise clients, I wasn't always able to interview or survey with our end user. I built out user personas as a way to consider user priorities in the design process.

I also conducted a Zoom user study with a few colleagues internally to test out new features early.

Optimizing a risk management tool

One of the successful projects I worked on was a risk management dashboard. Shipment managers check daily reports to track impacts on delivery timeliness — weather delays, a train strike, staffing shortages — for hundreds of shipments.

I collaborated with a team of product managers and engineers to build a tool that showed the impact of events like weather or strikes on a shipment path, all in a handy and actionable dashboard. This helped our client easily be able to diagnose problems with any shipments.
