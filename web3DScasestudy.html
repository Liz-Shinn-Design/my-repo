    The key problems

    No central system to coordinate design efforts

    Creating and maintaining a design system was needed to streamline the design process.

    No hub for design decisions

    Design decisions were frequently lost in team chat threads, and needed a central source of truth.

    Early research

    After researching the available open source design systems, I found that there weren't strong Web3 solutions. The UX Lead Sasha and I compiled notes on the top 10 systems we found.

    Our priority was a system that had a Figma community file, flexible code, well-maintained documentation, and an open source usage license. We decided to use Chakra and adopt an atomic Design System structure.

    I organized our Figma file and documentation into Theme elements (color, typography, grids, etc) and Component elements (atoms, molecules, organisms, etc.).

    Challenges we faced

    Engineering and Design had different priorities

    The engineering team prioritized extensive code docs, positive reviews from other engineers, and flexibility in customization. The design team prioritized visual maturity, active maintenance, and a robust existing Figma resource. After some debate, we landed on Chakra as the best solution for both design and engineering.

    Design Systems require maintenance

    As we introduced new features, we periodically needed to tend the design system. Myself and the UX Lead Sasha worked together to tend the design system as we added new features to the Threshold financial dashboard. We settled on a sprint schedule to coordinate our efforts.

    Design system results

    Using the design system helped us to work together as a design team more efficiently and to provide visually consistent designs to the engineering team.

    When new design hires joined the team, they benefited from the documentation and could quickly get up to speed.

    Open sourcing the system

    I packaged the system and published it to GitHub so that outside design teams could also benefit from our design research in the web3 space.
