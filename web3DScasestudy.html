Open Sourcing a Web3 Design System

Summary

As the UI Lead for Threshold Network, one of my design goals was to effectively scale our product design work. For the Threshold financial dashboard, we needed a design system. Since there weren't existing web3 design systems, we built our own, hosted it on Storybook, and open sourced it to Github.

My Approach

I started by researching the available open source design systems and compiling notes in Coda on the top 10 systems. I found that there were no robust Web3 solutions. The team needed a system that had a Figma community file, flexible code, well-maintained documentation, and an open source usage license.

After some debate, we landed on Chakra as the best solution for both design and engineering. I revised and rebranded the Community file for Chakra's design system to be for a financial Web3 dashboard. Myself and Sasha worked together to periodically tend system updates as the Threshold dashboard grew in complexity.

We faced some challenges initially with Chakra's dark mode and light mode when we started adding our own custom brand colors, and it did take some upfront engineering time for troubleshooting.

The Results

After maintaining the system through several feature launches, I had a robust resource for the UX/UI community in the Web3 space. So, I packaged up the system and published it to GitHub. My goal was to provide a design resource for internal teams across the Thesis studio working on other Web3 projects, as well as outside design teams that could also benefit from our design research.
