Playing for Keeps: DAOs & Design Systems

Summary

In the Web3 ecosystem, a DAO (Decentralized Autonomous Organization) is a loose organization of fans and users of a crypto protocol that congregate on a platform, usually on Discord. I led a project to build out a Keep DS coordinating with designers in the Keep DAO.

My Approach

I started first by facilitating an ideation workshop with the Keep design team. We brainstormed what we needed, what we thought there was expertise for, and what timeframe would be required.

I hadn't yet worked with the Keep DAO design community on a project, so this was new territory, with a lot of open questions. How would we handle feedback and accountability? How would we source talent that had the right expertise?

We determined that we get the best results by taking the time to prepare clear project parameters and expectations. Michael and I voted on the top three ideas, and I prepared detailed project briefs for those three prompts.

The Results

The Community Lead Will Rhodes launched the "Playing for Keeps" social campaign along with our design prompts.

Several designers from the DAO community volunteered to take on the design system project brief. I led the project over the course of several months with frequent feedback and collaboration. We had a component library internally at the start of the project, and by the end we had a full Keep design system.

This resource was a helpful blueprint for future design systems across the Thesis studio.
