    Early Thinking

    In the Web3 ecosystem, a DAO (Decentralized Autonomous Organization) is a loose organization of fans and users of a crypto protocol that congregate on a platform, usually on Discord.

    I started first by facilitating an ideation workshop with the Keep design team. We brainstormed what we needed, what we thought there was expertise for, and what timeframe would be required.

    In order to get the best results, I wanted to take the time to prepare clear project parameters and expectations. Michael and I voted on the top three ideas from our brainstorm, and I prepared detailed project briefs for those three prompts.

    The Challenges

    There weren't yet established guidelines for DAO collaboration.

    How would we handle feedback, accountability, and sourcing the right talent? For design, I was successful by writing project briefs, pinging back and forth in Figma, and hanging out in the Keep Discord regularly.

    Community contributors were often in different time zones or working other hours.

    Initially, I spent a substantial amount of time campaigning in the Keep DAO Discord talking to design contributors in the community. As the project continued, I reshuffled my availability and dialed back my presence to maintain a better cadence with other work.

    The Results

    The Community Lead Will Rhodes launched the "Playing for Keeps" social campaign along with our design prompts.

    Several designers from the DAO community volunteered to take on the design system project brief. I led the project over the course of several months with frequent feedback and collaboration. I had built a Keep component library internally at the start of the project, and by the end we had a fully built out Keep design system.

    This resource was a helpful blueprint for future design systems across the Thesis studio, and a best practice template for sourcing expertise from a project DAO.
