Embody Cycle Tracking App

Client: Embody

Overview
I was hired as the Product Design Lead for the Embody app to build a privacy-forward period tracking app that reflected what users actually want in their period tracking experience — to get helpful analysis, explainer tips about their cycle, and feel more in control.

Approach
At the outset, I started the team off with a workshop using the “Jobs to be Done” framework. In this workshop, the team and myself wrote down all of the potential tasks a user might want to accomplish using the Embody app. With this list, we built a set of key features for our MVP product. I used my experience tracking my own cycle as well as a design audit of leading period apps Clue and Flo. To supplement, I conducted a qualitative user study with 8 of our users that helped us to better make decisions about our product.

Design Process
As one example, I initiated the design process for building the app’s symptom logger with a user flow. I tested internally with a Figma wireframe prototype, and I discovered that users prioritized quickly logging symptoms. I used these findings to simplify with larger button selections, and custom icon imagery addressed the need for simplicity versus a text list of symptoms.

Design Systems
To leverage Figma components and be able to work quickly with the lead engineer, I built an Embody design system. This allowed me to scale and maintain consistency as we added product features.

Website Work
When I did an audit of existing websites in the space, I saw that it was important for Embody’s brand and business goals that we stood out from your typical generic stock photography. I contracted a brand photographer, and I directed a photo shoot where we snapped shots of Embody users using our app in real-world scenarios. It gave us an edge in the space, and the photos performed well in Embody pitch decks and on our social channels.
